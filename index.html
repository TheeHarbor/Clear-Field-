The Question

Iowa's cancer rates are rising.
We want to know why.

These are 2024 numbers. We're not saying we have the answer. We're saying the question deserves to be asked — and that Iowans deserve to be part of asking it.

#2

Source: Iowa Cancer Consortium, 2024
#1

Source: Iowa Cancer Consortium, 2024
9th

Source: Iowa Cancer Consortium, 2024
Sioux City. Pleasant Hill. Hawarden. Ankeny.
These Iowa zip codes are showing some of the highest localized cancer incidence rates in the state — clustered in regions with documented agricultural chemical runoff and aging water infrastructure. No one has proven the connection yet. That's what Clear Field is trying to find out.
Source: Des Moines Register / Iowa Cancer Consortium, Oct 2025

We don't know yet if Iowa's water is driving these cancer rates. That's exactly why we're asking. More data from more Iowans is how we start to find out.

Who We Are

Built by Iowans.
For Iowans.

Clear Field isn't a corporation. It's a citizen science network — built on three pillars.

Science

Every kit follows the same lab-certified testing protocol. Our data is open, our methods are transparent, and our results are peer-reviewable. We're not trying to prove a point — we're trying to find the truth.

Community

This map doesn't belong to us — it belongs to every family who's mailed in a sample. Over 240 Iowa households have tested their water. Their data is what makes Clear Field real.

Accountability

Data without action is just numbers. We publish every result. We share it with county health departments. And we fund efforts to hold legislators accountable for what's in Iowa's water.
